What is a Guitar?

The guitar is a stringed musical instrument that amplifies the vibration of its strings to create a tone.

The origin of the guitar is uncertain, although one of the earliest references to the instrument known as a guitar can be traced to 1200 A.D. Spain. It is likely that the guitar evolved from different instruments like the lute, oud, and other chordophones.

A chordophone is a stringed instrument that makes sound by vibrating one or multiple strings stretched between two points.
